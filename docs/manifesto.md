# The Isothermal Machian Universe
**A Trilogy of Research Notes**

## Abstract
This project explores a unified alternative to the standard $\Lambda$CDM cosmology. By postulating that **mass is a function of the Machian potential**, we derive three connected phenomena:
1.  **Cosmology:** The "expansion" of the universe is an illusion caused by the global evolution of mass scales ($m \propto t^{-1}$) in a static space.
2.  **Galaxies:** Flat rotation curves arise from a radial gradient in inertial mass ($m \propto r^{-1}$), eliminating the need for Dark Matter.
3.  **Black Holes:** Event horizons are phase transitions where time "freezes" ($\mathcal{F} \to 0$), creating a solid information storage surface.

---

### 1. [Galaxy Rotation](papers/paper_1_galaxy_rotation.pdf)

**Key Result:** Fits NGC 6503 rotation curve with $\chi^2_\nu \approx 0.94$ using parameters $R=0.89$ kpc, $\beta=0.98$.
**New:** Includes Baryonic Tully-Fisher derivation and Chameleon screening discussion.

### 2. [Cosmology](papers/paper_2_cosmology.pdf)
**Status:** Polished Draft
**Key Result:** Resolves the "Impossible Early Galaxies" problem by extending the age of the universe at high $z$.
**New:** Explicit Conformal Duality derivation and SN Ia luminosity distance analysis.

### 3. [Black Holes](papers/paper_3_black_holes.pdf)
**Status:** Polished Draft
**Key Result:** Interprets the Event Horizon as a "Vacuum Phase Transition" to a Solid State.
**New:** "Cold Firewall" resolution to the Information Paradox.

---

## Future Directions: Towards a Unified Field Theory
The current papers present a coherent set of heuristic models. The next phase of research must unify these under a single **Scalar-Tensor Action Principle**.

**The Goal:**
Write down a Lagrangian of the form:
$$ S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{R}{16\pi G} - \frac{1}{2}(\partial \phi)^2 - V(\phi) - m(\phi) \bar{\psi}\psi \right] $$
where the scalar field $\phi$:
1.  **Cosmology:** Rolls down the potential $V(\phi)$ over cosmic time, driving the global mass evolution $m(t)$.
2.  **Galaxies:** Develops spatial gradients $\nabla \phi$ in galactic potentials, creating the inertial mass profile $m(r)$.
3.  **Screening:** Acquires a large effective mass in high-density regions (Chameleon mechanism), satisfying Solar System constraints.

\begin{abstract}
We present two definitive "Smoking Gun" predictions of the Isothermal Machian Universe (IMU) that distinguish it from the standard $\Lambda$CDM + General Relativity paradigm. First, we show that the "Quantum Pressure" term inherent to the scalar field Lagrangian naturally resolves the "Cusp-Core Problem" by generating a repulsive force at small radii, converting density cusps into flat isothermal cores. Second, we demonstrate that the "Solid State" nature of the Machian event horizon implies that Black Holes are perfect reflectors of gravitational waves, predicting distinct post-merger "Echoes" detectable by LIGO. These signatures offer a clear path to falsification.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The standard model of cosmology ($\Lambda$CDM) faces two persistent crises on small scales:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item **The Cusp-Core Problem:** Simulations predict steep density cusps ($\rho \sim r^{-1}$) in dark matter halos, while observations reveal flat constant-density cores.
    \item **The Information Paradox:** The smooth event horizon of General Relativity implies information loss, violating quantum unitarity.
\end{enumerate}
We propose that these are not separate failures, but symptoms of the same underlying missing physics: the Machian scalar field.

\section{The Quantum Core}
In the IMU, the scalar field $\phi$ is governed by a Mimetic Lagrangian with a higher-derivative regulator $(\Box \phi)^2$. In the fluid limit, this term generates a "Quantum Pressure" $Q$:
\begin{equation}
    Q = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{\nabla^2 \sqrt{\rho}}{\sqrt{\rho}}
\end{equation}
This potential is negligible on large scales but dominates when density gradients are high.
For a NFW-like cusp $\rho \propto r^{-1}$, the Quantum Force scales as $F_Q \propto r^{-3}$, whereas gravity scales as $F_G \propto r^0$ (constant). Thus, at small radii, the repulsive Quantum Pressure inevitably overcomes gravity, halting collapse and forming a stable core.
